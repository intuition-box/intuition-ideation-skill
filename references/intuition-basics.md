# Intuition Protocol — Plain English Reference

Use this reference when explaining Intuition concepts to non-technical users.

## What is Intuition?

Intuition is a system that lets anyone create verifiable, public statements about anything — people, products, ideas, organizations — and back those statements with real money to show they mean it.

Think of it as a combination of Wikipedia (anyone can contribute knowledge), Yelp (people signal what's good), and a stock market (your signals have economic weight).

## Core Concepts (with analogies)

### Atoms
**What they are:** Building blocks. A permanent entry for any concept, person, thing, or idea.
**Analogy:** "Like a Wikipedia page, but for anything — and once created, it can never be deleted."
**Examples:** A person, a company, an idea, a product, a skill, a city.

### Triples
**What they are:** Connections between atoms. They follow the pattern: [Subject] → [Predicate] → [Object].
**Analogy:** "Like a sentence with exactly three parts: who/what, does what, to whom/what."
**Examples:**
- [Alice] → [recommends] → [Bob's Restaurant]
- [My Product] → [is built on] → [Intuition Protocol]
- [This Idea] → [solves] → [Trust Problem in Reviews]

### Staking / Signals
**What they are:** Putting tokens (money) behind an atom or triple to show you believe in it.
**Analogy:** "Putting your money where your mouth is. If you stake on something, you're publicly saying 'I believe this is true/valuable.'"
**Why it matters:** The more people stake on something, the more credible and visible it becomes. Early stakers get rewarded when others follow.

### Vaults
**What they are:** Every atom and every triple has its own "vault" — a pool where stakes are collected.
**Analogy:** "Like a tip jar for ideas. The fuller the jar, the more the community backs it."

### The Knowledge Graph
**What it is:** All the atoms and triples together form a giant web of knowledge.
**Analogy:** "Imagine a map where every idea, person, and thing is a dot, and the lines between them show how they're connected. That's the knowledge graph."

## Why It Matters for Ideas

When you publish an idea on Intuition:
1. **It becomes permanent** — recorded on-chain, can never be lost or censored
2. **It becomes discoverable** — anyone searching the knowledge graph can find it
3. **It can attract support** — people can stake on your idea to show they believe in it
4. **It can be built upon** — others can create new triples that connect your idea to other concepts
5. **Early belief is rewarded** — if you stake on an idea early and it gains traction, you benefit economically

## The Intuition Network

- Runs on its own blockchain (Layer 3, built on top of Base)
- Uses $TRUST tokens for staking
- All data is public and permanent
- Anyone can read the data; writing requires a small amount of $TRUST
